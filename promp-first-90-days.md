**What this does:** Builds a structured 30-60-90 day plan for your first three months in a new health tech engineering role — designed to help you build trust, ship early wins, and position yourself for the work that matters.

**How to use it:** Fill in what you know about the role and run it before or during your first week.

```
I'm starting a new engineering role at a health tech company. Help me build a clear 30-60-90 day plan for my first three months.

Here's the context:

- My role: [TITLE]
- Company: [NAME — and one sentence on what they do]
- Team size: [X engineers total / X on my immediate team]
- My specialty: [backend / full-stack / infrastructure / data / ML]
- What I was hired to do: [one to two sentences on the primary mandate]
- What I know about the current state of the codebase or systems: [what you learned in interviews — or "unknown"]
- My biggest concern about the first 90 days: [be honest]
- Company stage: [seed / Series A / Series B / later]

Please build a 30-60-90 day plan that covers:

Days 1-30 (Learn):
- What I should prioritize understanding before writing a single line of production code
- How to build relationships with the team, EM, and cross-functional partners in the first month
- What questions to ask in my first 1:1s with my manager and teammates
- What early observations I should be documenting

Days 31-60 (Contribute):
- How to identify the right first project or contribution — high visibility, low risk, meaningful
- How to demonstrate my value without stepping on existing ownership
- What to watch for as signals that I'm integrating well (or not)

Days 61-90 (Lead):
- How to start taking ownership of something — a system, a process, a problem
- How to have the right conversation with my manager about what success looks like at 6 months
- One thing I should have shipped or shipped a plan for by the end of day 90

End with: three questions I should bring to my manager at the end of day 30 to calibrate whether I'm on the right track.
```
