**What this does:** Writes a personalized cold outreach message to a specific hiring manager or engineering leader — short, human, and specific enough to get a response.

**How to use it:** Fill in what you know about the person and company, then run it in Claude. Edit before sending.

```
I want to reach out directly to a hiring manager or engineering leader at a health tech company I'm interested in. Help me write a short, human outreach message that gets a response.

Here's what I know:

- Their name: [NAME]
- Their role: [e.g., VP Engineering / Head of Platform / Engineering Manager]
- Company name: [COMPANY]
- What the company does: [one sentence]
- Something specific and real I know about them or the company: [e.g., they recently open-sourced their data pipeline / they just raised a Series B / the EM posted about their FHIR migration last week]
- My role level: [senior / staff / lead]
- My specialty: [backend / full-stack / infrastructure / data / ML]
- One sentence about my most relevant recent work: [e.g., I led the API layer rebuild for a telehealth platform processing 400K+ visits per month]
- Am I actively looking or building a relationship for later? [actively looking / building for later]

Write me a cold outreach message with the following constraints:
- 5-7 sentences maximum. No exceptions.
- Reference the specific thing I named about them or the company in the first two sentences.
- Don't list my skills or experience — summarize it in one sentence.
- Ask for something small: a 20-minute conversation, or to be on their radar.
- No apologies for reaching out. No "I hope this isn't too forward."
- End with my name and LinkedIn URL.

Also give me: one subject line if I'm sending via email, and one alternative version of the message if I'm sending via LinkedIn DM (slightly shorter, more conversational).
```
