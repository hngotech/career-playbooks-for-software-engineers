**What this does:** Builds a structured framework for evaluating a job offer — base, equity, bonus, benefits, growth trajectory, and the things most engineers forget to ask about before signing.

**How to use it:** Fill in the offer details and run it in Claude before accepting or negotiating anything.

```
I've received a job offer and I want to evaluate it carefully before responding. Help me think through the full picture.

Here are the offer details:

- Role: [TITLE]
- Company: [NAME]
- Stage: [seed / Series A / Series B / Series C+ / public]
- Base salary: [$X]
- Equity: [X shares / X% / $X grant — and what type: options, RSUs, SAFEs]
- Vesting schedule: [e.g., 4-year with 1-year cliff]
- Signing bonus: [$X or none]
- Annual bonus target: [X% or none]
- Benefits: [health, 401k match, PTO policy, remote/hybrid — what you know]
- Last valuation or share price (if known): [$X]
- My current compensation (if applicable): [$X base + $X equity]

Please give me:

1. Total compensation analysis — what this offer is actually worth annually and over 4 years, including realistic equity scenarios (conservative, base case, upside)
2. How this compares to market rate for this role level and stage (flag if it's below, at, or above market)
3. The five questions I should ask the company before accepting — about equity, growth, the team, and the role itself
4. What I should consider negotiating — and what's typically non-negotiable at this stage
5. Any red flags in the offer structure I should investigate further
6. A one-paragraph summary I can use to think through whether this is the right move — beyond just the compensation
```
