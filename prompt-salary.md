**What this does:** Generates a realistic compensation benchmark for your level, location, and specialty within tech — covering base salary, equity, and total comp at seed, Series A, and Series B+ companies.

**How to use it:** Upload or paste your resume into Claude, then paste the prompt below and run it. Use the output as your anchor before any compensation conversation.

---

## The prompt

```
# Compensation Benchmarking Prompt — Resume-Based

I'm a software engineer preparing for my next job search.

I've attached my resume below. Please use it to infer my profile — role level, years of experience, specialty, and location — and benchmark my compensation against current market data.

Based on my resume, please give me:

1. Your read of my profile — what level, specialty, and market you'd place me in, and why. Call out any ambiguities.

2. Base salary ranges for my profile at each company stage: seed, Series A, Series B, Series C+, and public. If my location affects ranges significantly, note city vs. remote differences.

3. Equity ranges at each stage — expressed as both a percentage of the company and an approximate dollar value assuming a reasonable exit scenario (e.g., $500M, $1B, $2B outcomes). Factor in typical vesting schedules and dilution.

4. Total compensation (base + equity + bonus) at each stage, with realistic floor, midpoint, and ceiling.

5. Health tech vs. other verticals — how my profile's comp would compare in fintech, enterprise SaaS, and consumer tech at the same level.

6. Three questions I should ask in any comp conversation to make sure I'm evaluating the full picture — not just the headline number.

7. Red flags to watch for in an offer at each company stage — specific to my level and the health tech market.

Be specific. Use real ranges, not vague guidance. If something on my resume makes me more or less competitive for a given stage or role type, tell me directly.
```

---

**Tips for best results:**

- Paste your resume as plain text directly into the prompt, or upload the file
- The more complete your resume (titles, dates, company names, tech stack), the more precise the benchmarking
- Add a line like *"I'm targeting Series B companies in NYC"* to narrow the output further
