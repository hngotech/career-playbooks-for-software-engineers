**What this does:** Builds a personalized, rehearsable answer to the most common interview opener — structured around your actual background, tailored to health tech, and calibrated to the specific role you're interviewing for.

**How to use it:** Fill in your details and run it in Claude. Then use the follow-up prompt at the bottom to practice.

```
I have an interview coming up at a health tech company. Help me build a strong, rehearsable answer to "Tell me about yourself."

Here's my background:

- Current or most recent role: [TITLE at COMPANY]
- What I built or owned in that role: [one to two sentences]
- Most relevant prior role: [TITLE at COMPANY — one sentence on what you did]
- The achievement I'm most proud of in the last 3 years: [describe it with specifics — what was the problem, what did you do, what was the result]
- Why I'm interested in health tech specifically: [your real reason — not a generic answer]
- What I'm looking for in my next role: [be specific — team size, problem type, stage, etc.]
- The role I'm interviewing for: [TITLE at COMPANY]
- What I know about the company: [one to two sentences about what they do and why it's interesting to you]

Please write me a "Tell me about yourself" answer that:
1. Runs 60-90 seconds when spoken aloud (roughly 150-200 words)
2. Opens with who I am and what I do — not where I went to school or how many years I've been coding
3. Names one specific achievement with real stakes — not a vague summary of my career
4. Connects my background to health tech specifically — not a generic pivot statement
5. Ends with why I'm interested in this company and this role in particular

After writing the answer, give me:
- A shorter version (30-45 seconds) for when I need to be brief
- Three follow-up questions the interviewer is likely to ask after I give this answer, and a one-sentence starter for each

Then ask me: "Want to practice? I'll play the interviewer — start whenever you're ready."
```

**Practice follow-up prompt (run this after the above):**

```
Let's practice. You are a senior engineering leader at a health tech company interviewing me for a Senior Engineering position. Start the interview with "Tell me about yourself" and then respond naturally to what I say — ask follow-up questions, probe on specifics, and give me honest feedback after each exchange on what landed and what I should sharpen.
```
